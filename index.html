Basic Question Types

Symbol: The symbol is the one or two letter abbreviation for an element, like H for Hydrogen or O for Oxygen.

Group: A group is a vertical column on the periodic table. Elements in the same group often behave similarly.

Period: A period is a horizontal row on the periodic table.

Category: A category describes what kind of element it is, such as Nonmetal, Halogen, or Noble Gas.

How to Find These

How to find the symbol: Look at the large letters inside the element box.

How to find the group: Count which vertical column the element is in. The group number is shown on the card too.

How to find the period: Count which horizontal row the element is in. The period number is shown on the card too.

How to find the category: Open the element card or use the color and placement of the element to help identify its family.

Element Families / Categories

Alkali Metals: These are very reactive metals in Group 1, like Lithium, Sodium, and Potassium.

Alkaline Earth Metals: These are reactive metals in Group 2, like Beryllium, Magnesium, and Calcium.

Transition Metals: These are the large block of metals in the center of the table, usually Groups 3 through 12.

Lanthanides: These are the upper detached row at the bottom of the table, elements 57 through 71.

Actinides: These are the lower detached row at the bottom of the table, elements 89 through 103.

Post-Transition Metals: These are metals to the right of the transition metals, including elements like Aluminum, Tin, and Lead.

Metalloids: These have properties between metals and nonmetals, such as Boron and Silicon.

Nonmetals: These include important elements like Hydrogen, Carbon, Nitrogen, Oxygen, Phosphorus, and Sulfur.

Halogens: These are very reactive nonmetals in Group 17, like Fluorine, Chlorine, and Bromine.

Noble Gases: These are the least reactive gases in Group 18, like Helium, Neon, and Argon.

Knowledge Questions

Knowledge questions are based on facts specific to each element, such as its uses, behavior, naming origin, or other important details. These are usually things you either already know or learn by reading the descriptions on each element card. Study carefully!
